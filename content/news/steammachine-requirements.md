Valve used its GDC 2026 slides to formalize the Steam Machine Verified badge: any game already Verified on Steam Deck will inherit the status on the console-style box.  
The performance floor is 30 frames per second at 1080p, reflecting the Machine’s higher power budget versus the handheld while keeping expectations clear for players.  
Input requirements mirror Steam Deck’s controller-first assumptions, but Valve won’t retest UI legibility or specific display resolutions, acknowledging TV play where text scaling is less critical.  
Valve reiterated that the Machine sits roughly six times above the Deck’s performance envelope, so most existing Verified titles should meet the target without developer tweaks.  
Steam Frame—the standalone VR sibling—gets a separate badge with a stricter 90 fps target for VR, but it doesn’t change the Machine’s baseline.
